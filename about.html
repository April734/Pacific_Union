About Us

Purpose

The organization, founded by Mrs. Vivian Boyce of Los Angeles, came about through her dream to unite the Bible workers of the Pacific Union. Sister Boyce saw the need of Bible Instructors coming together to encourage each other and also to inspire new workers to enter this phase of God’s work. In 1978, Sister Boyce commenced this dream with less than 15 members in the organization. It was her purpose also to lift the Bible Instructor’s work higher in the eyes of God’s people so that others could see the seriousness of such a task. The organization is to be ongoing in its nature and scope.

Goal

It is the supreme goal of this organization to uplift Christ and prepare individuals for His return. To do so, the members recruit Lay Bible Instructors, train workers, fellowship with one another to realize kindred spirits, discuss problems, and seek the best ways to make their work as Bible Instructors more effective. Thus, through sharing, caring, fellowship, and planning, the members seek to accomplish their primary goal.

BYLAWS

MEETINGS

Annual Meeting - A yearly face to face annual meeting shall be held on the Bible Instructor's Day, in January on a MLK weekend. The place to be decided one year before on a Conference Chapter rotation basis. Written notice of the date, time and place of the meeting shall be served via U.S. mail or email, not less than thirty (30) days before the meeting, upon such persons whose name appear on the books of the Pacific Union Bible Instructors' Guild as members.

Monthly Meetings

The Pacific Union Bible Instructors' Guild began conducting monthly telephone conference call meetings in the year of 2013. These meetings to be held on the first Sunday of each month. A yearly meeting will be held at the Pastor's and Workers Care Retreat.


Chapter meetings may be held at the discretion of the Chapter Leaders and members, as needed, to help organize workers in their area and make monthly reports at the Bible Instructors' Guild Meeting.

Quorum

Fourteen (14) of the Guild officers and Chapter Presidents shall constitute a quorum at any meeting of the membership.

Organizational Structure

The Pacific Union Bible Instructor's Guild will have the following organizational structure.

The officers of the Bible instructors' Guild shall be: President, Vice President, Recording Secretary, Correspondence Secretary, Treasurer, Assistant Treasurer, Chaplain and Parliamentarian.

Elections

Elections of Officers - will occur at the annual meeting of the Guild every two years. Voting privileges shall be restricted to paid members of the Guild.

Membership Eligibility

Individuals eligible for membership must be Seventh Day Adventist members in good and regular standing and approved by their church pastor. Active members should demonstrate the ability to win one soul to Christ per year.

Rights of Members

It is the right of each member to make suggestions and be heard by the group. It is the right of each member to call a question on anything that is not clear.

New Members

All new members must be voted in by a majority vote after completing the application process and submit dues.

Voting

Each member has one vote on any issue which is being voted upon by the Pacific Union Bible Instructors' Guild.

OFFICERS

President

The President will preside at the annual and monthly meetings, call for the secretary's and treasurer's report, along with any other report. President will be responsible for compiling and distributing the agenda prior to the meetings. President will work closely with the Secretary and Treasurer to see that all business of the organization is done. In the event of a tie at meetings the President has the right to vote upon the question. The President shall communicate to the Pacific Union Bible Instructors' Guild such matters as may in his/her opinion promote its welfare. The President shall perform as required by the nature of that office and shall enforce the Bylaws, rules and regulations of the Guild. The President shall appoint officers and other members of authorized committees and shall be an ex-officio members of elections. President will also report, periodically, to the Pacific Union Bible Instructors' Coordinator.

Vice President

In the absence of the President, the Vice-President shall preside. In the absence of both the President and the Vice President, an Acting Chair shall be chosen by a majority vote for that meeting. The Vice President will function as an assistant to the President and work closely with the other officers of the Bible Instructors' Guild.

Recording Secretary

The Recording Secretary shall keep a record of the proceedings of the meetings of the Pacific Union Bible Instructors' Guild. The record of meetings proceeding shall be made available to the officers and members within a reasonable time.


If the Secretary is absent from a meeting, an acting secretary shall be appointed by the President, Vice President or Acting Chair for that meeting.

Correspondence Secretary

Correspondence Secretary shall issue all notices of meetings of the membership and be responsible for all correspondence pertaining to the affairs of the Guild. This includes technology, distribution of minutes, newsletter, social media, and rally program booklet, except as otherwise provided.

Treasurer/Assistant Treasurer

The Treasurer shall collect and have custody of all funds of the Guild, and disperse them under the direction of the Executive Committee and/or President. The treasurer may reimburse any member who presents a bill whenever approved by President or Executive Committee. The Treasurer shall keep the accounts of the Guild and shall make monthly reports of its financial condition to the President and Assistant Treasurer by the fifteenth (15th) of each month. The Treasurer shall cause to have performed an annual financial review of the records of the Guild.


The annual review shall be conducted by a three (3) member elected committee as specified in the section on committees. The President or the Vice President and the Treasurer shall sign all written contracts and obligations of the Guild. All checks drawn upon the Guild funds over $500.00, shall be signed by the President or Vice President, and counter signed by another officer. Neither the President nor any other officer of the Guild shall have the authority or power to make the Guild liable for any debt beyond the amount of funds which may be, at the time, in the Treasury. All outstanding debt shall be paid first. The Treasury records may be inspected by any member at any time upon request.

Chaplain

The Chaplain shall be responsible for prayer, devotional thought and all of the spiritual activities during the Bible instructors' Guild meetings.

Parliamentarian

The Parliamentarian shall be responsible for the rules and procedures of Parliamentarian law; advise the presiding officer on questions of procedure in transacting the business of the Guild legally, efficiently and impartially, and help to draft bylaws and resolutions. All Bible Instructors' Guild meetings shall be governed in Parliamentary procedure by Roberts Rules of Order.
